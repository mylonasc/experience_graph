## Context

Electricity prices and dispatch arise from the interaction of generator costs, renewable availability, transmission constraints, ramping behavior, and market design. A useful ML test bed therefore has to preserve the physical and economic structure of the grid.

## Contribution

- Built simulations of generator dispatch across the European grid while accounting for generation mix, network constraints, and marginal bidding costs.
- Produced animated geospatial views of wind, combined-cycle gas, and oil generation over time.
- Analyzed day-ahead, intraday, and balancing-market mechanics alongside merit-order and zonal-pricing concepts.
- Framed deterministic and stochastic market-clearing approaches as a foundation for future forecasting and uncertainty-aware ML experiments.

## Outcome

The project created a practical bridge between power-system optimization, energy-market reasoning, and machine-learning experimentation, supported by visual explanations for non-specialist audiences.
