## Context

Small cracks can create highly localized strain patterns whose effect decays rapidly with distance, while real sensor layouts are sparse and irregular.

## Contribution
- Project conception, project planning, project execution, validation
- Represented arbitrarily positioned strain sensors and their relations as a graph.
- Combined Graph Neural Networks with scalable Bayesian weight uncertainty techniques to estimate a distribution over crack position rather than a single point prediction.
- Generated a simulation dataset of a hollow tube under random dynamic excitations with varying crack geometry and orientation.
- Validated the method for strain-based damage localization and communicated uncertainty in the inferred location.

## Outcome

The research showed how geometric deep learning and Bayesian inference can handle irregular sensing layouts for uncertainty-aware structural diagnostics.
