## Context

Local organizations needed a way to communicate changes in public health indicators without requiring readers to interpret raw spreadsheets.

## Contribution

- Combined open datasets from public agencies and normalized inconsistent region identifiers.
- Designed annotated charts that explained spikes, missing values, and reporting delays.
- Added plain-language summaries for non-technical readers.
- Reviewed color contrast, keyboard navigation, and chart captions.

## Outcome

The dashboard was used in public briefings and community updates, with an emphasis on transparency and uncertainty.
